Cloud Cover by Latitude

There is no apparent correlation between latitude and cloud cover. Cloudiness is a function not only of local light intensity and temperatures but also of topography, wind direction, humidity, and movement of air masses from other areas. The most noticable trend is the clustering of values at certain round values (such as 100%, 90%, 75%, 40%, etc) across all latitudes. This is possibly due to the data being entered at many weather stations as an eyeball estimate rather than a measured value of how much of the sky is covered by cloud.
